Metaverse

The oxford dictionary defines metaverse as “a virtual-reality space in which users can interact with a computer-generated environment and other users.” To be more precise, Metaverse is a compound word of transcendence meta and universe and refers to a three-dimensional virtual world where avatars engage in political, economic, social, and cultural activities with blockchain being its backbone. It is widely used in the sense of a virtual world based on daily life where both the real and the unreal coexist. The scope of the Metaverse is wide and continuously growing. In previous studies, Metaverse focused on the composition of the virtual world itself (e.g., game), but recently, it is often expressed as a medium for exchanging interests and social interaction centred on content.

NFT

NFTs or non-fungible tokens are non-exchangeable digital assets that can be anything from a simple jpeg file to music to even a tweet. In recent months NFTs have taken the world by storm. The ownership of a certain NFT is recorded in the blockchain and that person is regarded as the owner of that digital asset and gets exclusive ownership rights. NFTs can have only one owner at a time. NFTs’ unique data makes it easy to verify their ownership and transfer tokens between owners. The owner or creator can also store specific information inside them. For instance, artists can sign their artwork by including their signature in an NFT’s metadata. Collectible items can also be traded as NFTs and this can be used in online environments, like games for instance.
